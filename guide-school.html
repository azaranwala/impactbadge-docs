School Admin & Teacher Guide

Manage student compliance, track graduation hours, and run reports

Two Roles — School Admin vs Teacher

🏫 School Admin (Counselor)

  • Full access to all dashboard features
  • Set graduation hour requirements
  • Approve/reject pre-approval requests
  • Bulk import student rosters
  • Configure school settings
  • Generate district reports
  • Manage Clever Sync connection
  • Register school domains
  • Generate Partner API keys
  • Send school-wide announcements
  • View COPPA compliance dashboard
  • Export student data (FERPA requests)
  • Restrict / unrestrict student accounts

👩‍🏫 Teacher (Advisor)

  • Read-only student list with hours
  • View compliance status
  • Export student data as CSV
  • See school-wide summary stats
  • Cannot change requirements
  • Cannot bulk import
  • Cannot access settings
  • Cannot verify hours

Setting Graduation Requirements

1. Go to Settings

School Dashboard → Settings page.

2. Set Graduation Minimum Hours

e.g., 75 hours for graduation, 10 hours per grade per year.

3. Set Deadlines

Senior deadline (e.g., May 1), quarterly checkpoints.

4. Configure Categories

Which volunteer categories count toward graduation (CSL categories for HCPS).

5. Save

Settings are saved and apply immediately. Students see their progress bar update.

Settings Persist: When you return to the Settings page, your saved values are pre-loaded automatically.

Monthly Graduation Nudges (Automatic)

On the 1st of every month, ImpactBadge automatically:

  1. Checks every student's progress against graduation requirements
  2. Sends push notifications to at-risk and below-minimum students
  3. Notifies linked parents of behind students
  4. Emails the school admin a compliance digest with counts and a CSV attachment

Clever Sync (Roster Management)

If your district uses Clever Complete, student and teacher rosters sync automatically:

  • Daily full sync at 2:00 AM EST — students, teachers, staff (counselors), sections, schools
  • Every 15 minutes — incremental changes (new students, class transfers, teacher reassignments, removals)
  • History preserved — every school transfer, grade change, class change, and teacher reassignment is automatically recorded before the profile is updated. Nothing is overwritten without a snapshot being saved first.
  • Removals — students removed from Clever are archived (not deleted). Their hours, history, and badges are preserved.
  • Restorations — if a student reappears in Clever, they're automatically restored with all historical data intact.

View sync status at /school/clever — see counts, last sync time, and trigger a manual sync.

Year-End Setup: Go to Settings → Year-End Date and enter your last day of school (e.g., 05-31 for May 31). ImpactBadge will automatically archive sections and create compliance snapshots on that date every year — no manual action needed.

Bulk Student Import (CSV)

Import your student roster without Clever:

  1. Prepare a CSV with columns: Email, Name, Grade (optional), Student ID (optional), Graduation Year (optional)
  2. Go to Import Students → upload the CSV (max 500 rows)
  3. Existing students are updated; new students are pre-provisioned (applied when they sign up)

1. Student Submits Request

Student fills in: organization name, planned activity, estimated hours, planned dates.

2. Admin Reviews

Go to Approvals → see pending pre-approvals. Review the organization and activity.

COPPA Compliance

Access at: impacthub-prod.web.app/school/coppa

The Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) requires verified parental consent before students under 13 can use ImpactBadge. The COPPA dashboard shows every under-13 student at your school and their consent status.

Consent Statuses

StatusMeaningAction Required
✅ GrantedParent approved via email linkNone — student is active
⏳ PendingConsent email sent, awaiting parent responseAccount is automatically locked until granted
❌ RejectedParent clicked "I do not consent"Restrict the account; student cannot use the app
⌛ Expired7-day consent link expired before parent respondedRestrict until new consent obtained

Account States

  • Active — consent granted, student can fully use the app
  • Locked — automatic hold from parentalConsentGranted: false (set at signup). Student sees "waiting for parent approval" screen in the app.
  • Restricted — manual hold placed by school admin (via Restrict button). Used when consent is rejected or expired and the automatic lock needs reinforcement.
Note: Most under-13 students don't need manual restriction — the automatic consent lock prevents them from acting until a parent approves. Use the Restrict button only when consent has been rejected or expired and you need to enforce the hold manually.

How the Consent Flow Works

1. Student Signs Up

Student enters grade 6 or 7 during signup. App prompts for parent/guardian email.

2. Consent Email Sent

ImpactBadge sends a COPPA consent email to the parent. Includes what data is collected, parent rights, and approve/reject buttons. Link expires in 7 days.

3. Parent Responds

Parent clicks Approve → student account is unlocked. Parent clicks Reject → student is notified and account stays locked.

4. Counselor Monitors

COPPA page shows all under-13 students and consent status. Counselor can restrict accounts for rejected/expired cases.

Parent Rights Under COPPA

  • Right to review what data ImpactBadge has collected on their child
  • Right to request deletion of their child's data — contact privacy@impactbadge.app
  • Right to revoke consent at any time — contact privacy@impactbadge.app; school admin should then restrict the account

FERPA — Student Data Access Requests

The Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA) gives parents (and students 18+) the right to access education records. Since volunteer hours tracked in ImpactBadge are used for graduation compliance, they are considered education records once the school uses them officially.

When a Parent Submits a FERPA Request

1. Parent contacts the school

Parent submits a written FERPA data request to the counselor or registrar.

2. Counselor opens the student's detail panel

Go to Compliance → click the student's name.

3. Click FERPA Export

Downloads a complete JSON file of all ImpactBadge data for that student.

4. Provide to parent within 45 days

FERPA requires schools to respond within 45 days of a request.

What the FERPA Export Contains

SectionContents
ProfileName, email, grade, school, district, graduation year, student ID, state ID, total hours, badges, interests
Hour LogsEvery event attended with hours, date, organization, and verification status
Self-Reported HoursAll self-reports with supervisor name/email, description, signature, photos, status
Parental ConsentsAll COPPA consent records with parent email, status, timestamps
SkillsAll skills on the student's profile
ReflectionsAll post-event reflections submitted by the student
School HistoryComplete record of every school, district, and grade the student has been in — with dates, hours at departure, and reason for transition (school transfer, district transfer, grade promotion)
Class / Section HistoryEvery class the student was ever enrolled in — section name, subject, period, grade level, all teachers assigned, and the full log of any teacher changes while the student was in that class
Audit Trail: Every FERPA export is automatically logged (ferpaExportLog) with the admin's name, timestamp, and student. School history and class history are always included, so the export is a complete picture of the student's entire time in ImpactBadge — even across multiple schools and districts.

Student History Tracking

ImpactBadge automatically preserves a complete, permanent audit trail for every student — across schools, teachers, classes, grades, and districts. Nothing is ever deleted. The student's volunteer hours, badges, and XP remain tied to their account for life.

No Action Required

All history is captured automatically by the daily Clever sync (2AM) and the 15-minute real-time event poll. You do not need to take any action for history to be recorded — it happens the moment Clever reports a change.

Year-End Rollover

Configure your school's year-end date once in Settings → Year-End Date (e.g., May 31). On that date, ImpactBadge automatically:

  • Archives all current Clever sections for your school (they disappear from active views)
  • Creates a compliance snapshot for every student — recording their total hours, grade, and on-track status at year-end
  • Sends an in-app notification to school admins confirming the rollover completed

Student volunteer hours are never reset — cumulative totals carry forward. Only the active class roster is archived.

Viewing a Student's History

The full history is available in two places:

  • FERPA Export — the downloaded JSON includes the complete school history and class/section history with teacher change logs
  • Firestore (for technical staff) — stored in users/{uid}/schoolHistory and users/{uid}/sectionMemberships subcollections
Why this matters: If a parent questions a student's compliance record after a school transfer, or a district auditor asks which teacher supervised a student in a given semester, ImpactBadge has the complete answer — automatically, with exact dates.

Teacher Advisor Dashboard

Teachers see a simplified read-only dashboard at /advisor:

  • Stats cards: total students, total hours, on-track percentage
  • Searchable student list with hours, grade, and compliance status
  • Export student data as CSV
  • No write access — cannot modify settings, import students, or verify hours

LMS Connect (Canvas / Schoology)

If your district has Clever LMS Connect enabled:

  1. Teachers can create volunteer hour assignments that appear in the LMS gradebook
  2. Students complete hours in ImpactBadge
  3. Grades auto-sync — hours are converted to points: (hours completed / required hours) × points possible
  4. Students can launch ImpactBadge directly from Canvas/Schoology via LTI 1.3
