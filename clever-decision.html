Clever SSO Integration

Decision Document for Co-Founder Review

1. Executive Summary

ImpactBadge needs school SSO integration so that students and teachers at K-12 schools can sign in using their school credentials without creating separate accounts. Clever is the dominant SSO provider in K-12, used by over 100,000 schools and 25 million students in the US.

This document presents two integration options, their trade-offs, costs, data implications, and a recommendation for co-founder approval before proceeding.

Decision Required: Which Clever integration type should ImpactBadge adopt?
  • Option A: Clever Library — Free, teacher-driven adoption, limited student data
  • Option B: Clever Complete (District SSO) — Paid, district-driven, full student data

2. What is Clever?

Clever is a middleware platform that connects educational software to school districts' Student Information Systems (SIS). It provides:

  • Single Sign-On (SSO): Students and teachers log in with their school credentials (one click)
  • Rostering: Automatically syncs student/teacher data from the school's SIS
  • Data Privacy: Acts as a trusted intermediary, ensuring FERPA/COPPA compliance

Market position: Used by 89 of the 100 largest US school districts. Competitors include ClassLink (25M students) and Google Classroom.

6. Recommendation

Recommended: Start with Clever Library, upgrade to Complete later.

Rationale:

  1. Speed to market: Clever Library can go live in 1-2 weeks vs. 2-6 months for Complete. We need schools using ImpactBadge NOW.
  2. Zero cost: No financial commitment. No revenue needed to fund the integration.
  3. Teacher-driven adoption: Matches our go-to-market strategy — counselors and teachers adopt ImpactBadge for their students without IT department gatekeeping.
  4. HCPS compatible: Teachers at HCPS can start using ImpactBadge immediately.
  5. Data minimization: Less student data = less privacy risk = simpler compliance.
  6. Upgrade path clear: Once we prove value with Library, districts will request Complete for full rostering. We can add it later without disrupting existing users.

7. ImpactBadge Data Ownership Model

Core Principle: Students own their data. ImpactBadge accounts belong to the student, not the school.

How This Works:

ScenarioWhat Happens
Student signs up with personal email (john@gmail.com)Account created with personal email as primary. Student owns this account.
Student later logs in via Clever at schoolClever authenticates student. ImpactBadge looks up existing account. If found, links Clever to existing account. Personal email preserved.
Student graduates / leaves schoolStudent keeps their account and all data. Clever SSO stops working, but personal email login still works. All hours, badges, exports remain.
Teacher views student on dashboardTeacher sees student's name, school, total hours, compliance status. Teacher does NOT see personal email, messages, or account credentials.

With Clever Library specifically:

  • Clever does NOT share student email — so ImpactBadge never receives the school email
  • Student's personal email (used at signup) remains the ONLY email on the account
  • This perfectly aligns with "student owns their data"
  • If student never signed up before and logs in via Clever first, they'll be prompted to enter a personal email after Clever login
